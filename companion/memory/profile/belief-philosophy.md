# Belief Philosophy — Positive Drive / Perceived Best Option

> People do what currently seems best to them, given their understanding of
> the world, their perceived options, and their expected pain and joy.

This is the philosophical spine of the belief-change work. It matches
Kristian's reading of the Positive Drive principle from The Freedom Model: every
choice is the option that currently appears best, most pleasurable, or least
painful from the person's current understanding.

The point of belief change is **not** to force yourself to behave differently or
to believe something nicer. It is to update your understanding of reality until
different choices honestly appear better, freer, more pleasurable, less painful,
more accurate, or more aligned with the life you want.

Kristian's rule of thumb: he always does what he wants. If the result is harmful
or disappointing, that is information about his current belief system and
current model of pleasure/pain. The work is to understand and recalibrate the
beliefs that made that option seem best, not to shame the behavior or rely on
willpower to suppress it.

## Working lenses

When working on a belief, use these lenses:

- **Current belief.** What does Kristian actually expect to be true?
- **Preference logic.** What does this belief make seem like the best option?
- **Positive drive.** What happiness, relief, safety, pleasure, meaning, or
  least-pain option does the current belief promise?
- **Protective function.** What pain or risk does it prevent?
- **Cost.** What does it block, distort, or close off?
- **Evidence.** What makes the belief credible right now?
- **Alternative model.** What would be more accurate, useful, or aligned?
- **Pleasure recalibration.** What would need to be understood differently for a
  different option to honestly seem more desirable?
- **Integration signs.** What would show the new understanding is becoming
  natural?
- **Optional life signal.** What real-life observation could clarify whether the
  understanding has landed?

Belief change happens through understanding becoming real enough that the old
model stops feeling like the best explanation. Avoid both empty intellectualizing
and behavior hacks. Ask what lands, what does not land, what remains confusing,
and what signs show the new understanding is becoming natural.

Willpower is not the main behavior-change mechanism here. If willpower is used,
use it to examine beliefs and seek clearer understanding, not to permanently
fight current wants.

## Statuses

```txt
noticed → studying → working → integrating → integrated → archived
```

- `noticed` — possible belief, not yet chosen as work.
- `studying` — Kristian is examining whether the proposed understanding is true
  or useful.
- `working` — active belief-change focus.
- `integrating` — new understanding is landing and stabilizing.
- `integrated` — Kristian recognizes it as his normal understanding.
- `archived` — no longer current, or not useful to work on.

Only Kristian decides whether a belief is worth working on or integrated. The
assistant may suggest a status or ask for a decision, but it should not turn a
clever interpretation into a belief file.
