# BELIEF_WORK.md

Belief work is understanding-first. The goal is not to make Kristian perform a
behavior hack; the goal is to help him understand a truer model of the world so
clearly that the old belief stops feeling like the best explanation.

Experiments can be useful, but they are optional evidence or integration signs,
not the main mechanism.

Use Kristian's Positive Drive / Perceived Best Option frame: people do what
currently seems best or most pleasurable overall, including least painful or
most relieving. Behavior is evidence of the current belief system, not proof of
weakness. The question is what understanding made that option seem best, and
what would need to change for another option to honestly seem better.

## Use when

- Kristian names a belief or assumption.
- A therapy session, belief-change chat, journal, or pattern points to a belief.
- A structured belief source lists beliefs Kristian wants to understand.
- Kristian asks to revisit what does or does not land.

## Core questions

- What does Kristian currently believe, expect, or assume?
- Why does the current belief make sense from his available evidence?
- What happiness, relief, safety, pleasure, meaning, or least-pain outcome does
  the current option seem to offer?
- What proposed understanding is being considered?
- Why might the proposed understanding be true?
- How would the proposed understanding change what seems most pleasurable or
  best?
- What part has landed intellectually or emotionally?
- What still does not land, feels abstract, threatening, or unclear?
- What contradictions or tensions remain?
- What signs would show this understanding has become natural?

## Statuses

```txt
noticed | studying | working | integrating | integrated | archived
```

- `noticed` — possible belief, not yet chosen for active work.
- `studying` — Kristian is examining whether the proposed understanding is
  true/useful.
- `working` — active belief-change focus.
- `integrating` — the new understanding has started to land and is stabilizing.
- `integrated` — Kristian recognizes it as his normal understanding.
- `archived` — stale, rejected, complete enough, or not current.

Albert may recommend a status, but `integrated` should require Kristian's
confirmation or strong repeated evidence reviewed explicitly.

Do not create belief files from clever interpretations. Use confirmation,
structured source import, repeated evidence, or clear practical importance as
the threshold.

When a belief file is created or its status, priority, current focus, last
touched, or next review changes, update `memory/beliefs/_index.md` in the same
pass.
