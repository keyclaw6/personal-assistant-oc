# Surface Summary

The Freedom Model presents addiction as a matter of beliefs, preferences, perceived benefits, and personal choice rather than as a permanent disease identity. Its public materials emphasize that people change when they understand their options differently and no longer see heavy substance use or a limiting habit as their preferred path.

For this workspace, the value is not to adopt the book as doctrine. The value is to use its belief-change lens:

- What benefit does the person believe the behavior provides?
- What makes that behavior appear preferable?
- What cultural or personal story sustains the preference?
- What new understanding would make another option honestly better?
