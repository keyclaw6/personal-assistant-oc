# Daily-Life Implications

If I fully understood this model, then in normal life I would:

- Ask what benefit I believe a behavior gives me before trying to stop it.
- Compare options by honest perceived value, not by moral pressure alone.
- Notice when identity labels make a behavior feel inevitable.
- Look for information that changes preference.
- Treat urges, habits, and avoidance as meaningful preference signals to inspect.
- Design experiments that test whether the old option is actually as beneficial as it seems.
- Avoid shame as a change method because shame can make escape behaviors look more valuable.
